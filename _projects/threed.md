**Roles:** 
- Sandve, project lead
- Hvidsten, WP-lead

Whole genome duplications (WGD) are among the most dramatic events in genome evolution, doubling every gene and regulatory element in a single step. This project investigates how WGD reshapes the three-dimensional organization of genomes — including topologically associating domains, compartments, and enhancer–promoter contacts — and what consequences this reorganization has for gene regulation and adaptation.

Using Atlantic salmon as a model system (which underwent a WGD ~80 million years ago), we combine Hi-C chromatin conformation capture, ATAC-seq, and comparative genomics to trace how 3D genome architecture evolves after polyploidy.
